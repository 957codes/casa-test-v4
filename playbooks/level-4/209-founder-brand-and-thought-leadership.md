# Founder Brand and Thought Leadership

## Procedure
1. Pick one primary platform where your ICP already reads (usually X or LinkedIn) and one owned backstop you control (an email list). Reserve the handle, write a one-line bio that states who you help and the contrarian belief you hold, and link to a single subscribe page.
2. Define three to five content pillars from your positioning: the problem you obsess over, what the category gets wrong, behind-the-scenes building, customer outcomes, and a personal angle. Write each pillar as a sentence you could defend in an argument. Save these as content_pillars.
3. Set a concrete publishing_cadence and put it on the calendar: three short posts and one longer piece (thread, essay, or teardown) per week. Batch-draft a two-week backlog now so a busy week never breaks the streak.
4. Ship the first week immediately. For each post, open with a specific claim, give one concrete example or number, and end with a question or invitation. Reply to every comment within the first hour to compound reach.
5. Convert attention into an asset: add one clear call to join the email list in your bio and pinned post, and send a weekly note to subscribers. This builds owned_distribution_list independent of any algorithm.
6. Every Friday, review reach, replies, and new subscribers. Double down on the two posts that outperformed, cut the format that flopped, and refill the backlog.

## Output
A live founder_audience growing weekly across one public platform and one owned list, with a documented cadence and pillar set.
