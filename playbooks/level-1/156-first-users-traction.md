# First Users by Hand

A consumer product with no users does not have a marketing problem yet. It has a
"nobody has tried this" problem. The fix is to do things that do not scale: go to the
three places your audience already gathers and recruit the first cohort one by one. A
hundred users you talked to teach you more than ten thousand from a paid burst.

## Procedure

1. Name the 3 to 5 communities, subreddits, Discords, group chats, or local spots
   where the target audience already gathers (from the beachhead).
2. Pick the hand-to-hand tactic per channel: a genuinely useful post, direct messages,
   a waitlist with a reason to share, or showing up in person. No ads yet.
3. Recruit the first cohort by hand. Onboard them yourself and watch where they get
   stuck.
4. Record what they did and where they dropped. Use it to fix the first-run experience
   before recruiting the next cohort.

## Output

A `first_users` cohort and an `early_user_feedback` loop, written to the company brain.
This unblocks activation, retention, and any later paid channel (you now know what
converts).

## Rules

- Do not buy what you have not earned. Paid acquisition before you can convert and
  retain a hand-recruited cohort just rents you churn.
- Go where they already are. Recruiting from the wrong community gives you users who
  never belonged and a misleading read on demand.
- Talk to every one of the first cohort. The point of this stage is learning, not a
  vanity signup count.
