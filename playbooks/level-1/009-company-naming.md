# Company Naming

The name is the single most durable piece of IP a business creates and is rarely
changed without large cost. Pass four filters: linguistically strong,
strategically aligned, legally available, and digitally acquirable.

## Procedure

1. Write the naming brief from the positioning and beachhead audience.
2. Generate candidates at scale across naming types (descriptive, suggestive,
   arbitrary, coined).
3. Screen linguistically and phonetically (easy to say, spell, recall; no negative
   meanings in target languages).
4. Apply the SMILE and SCRATCH filters; run a trademark knockout and domain or
   handle availability pre-check.
5. Score the survivors and present a ranked shortlist to the founder for the final
   pick (human gate).

## Output

`brand_name` (the selected name) and `name_shortlist`, written to the company
brain. Unlocks domain acquisition (010), trademark clearance (011), and entity
naming (012).

## Rules

- Naming after the beachhead, never before; a name optimized for the wrong
  audience is wasted work.
- Final selection requires explicit founder approval. Hard to reverse later.
- Do not lock a name whose exact-match domain and core trademark class are both
  blocked.
