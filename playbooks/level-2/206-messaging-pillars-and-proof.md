# Messaging Pillars and Proof

## Procedure
1. Pull your locked positioning statement and underline the 3 to 4 claims a buyer must believe for the positioning to hold. Each underlined belief becomes a candidate pillar. Name each pillar as a short, plain declarative sentence written from the customer's point of view, not an internal feature label.
2. For every pillar, list proof points that make it credible: a metric, a named customer outcome, a demo behavior, a third party validation, a guarantee, or a mechanism that explains why the claim is true. Aim for at least two concrete proofs per pillar. Mark any pillar with weak or missing proof.
3. Stress test each proof against a skeptic. Strip adjectives like fast, simple, or powerful and replace them with evidence a buyer can verify. If a claim cannot be backed, either downgrade it to a supporting line or open a proof gap to close with data, a case study, or a benchmark.
4. Assemble the messaging house: positioning anchor on top, pillars as the columns, proof points stacked under each. Add a one line headline and one supporting sentence per pillar that copywriters can lift verbatim.
5. Validate by drafting one homepage hero and one cold outreach opener using only sentences from the house. If you reach for a claim not in the house, it is missing a pillar or a proof. List remaining proof gaps with an owner and a date.

## Output
A messaging_house document with 3 to 4 proof backed message_pillars and a ranked list of proof_points to acquire.
