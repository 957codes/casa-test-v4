# Pre-Sales and Letters of Intent

## Procedure
1. Pull 15 to 25 named accounts from your target account list that already confirmed the problem in validation calls. Rank by reachability and budget authority, then pick the top 10 to work this week.
2. Draft a one-page LOI template with five fields: the problem it solves, the scope of the pilot, the price or paid-pilot fee, the start window, and a non-binding intent clause. Keep it short enough to sign without legal review. Prepare a second variant that is a paid-pilot agreement with a real invoice.
3. Build a tiered ask. Lead with a paid pilot. If the buyer hesitates on cash before the build, fall back to a signed LOI committing to evaluate at a defined milestone. Always anchor to a date and a number, never to vague interest.
4. Run the outreach: book 10 conversations, present the scope and price, and ask for signature on the call or within 72 hours. Send the document during the call, not after.
5. Log every account in an LOI pipeline tracker with status, objection, price discussed, and next date. Review conversion daily and adjust the offer if more than half stall on the same objection.
6. Convert at least 3 signatures or paid pilots into a design partner roster you will build against.

## Output
A signed LOI or paid-pilot set plus a pipeline tracker showing documented pre-revenue demand.
