# LinkedIn Organic

Founder-led content that positions the founder as the category authority and
builds a compounding inbound pipeline. People buy from people they trust; trust is
built long before the product page. Every post must pass the "would a real founder
say this?" test.

## Procedure

1. Confirm prerequisites. Defined ICP and buyer persona, a documented Spiky Point
   of View, an optimized profile, approved content pillars, and a self-reported
   attribution field ("how did you hear about us?") live on the site.
2. Define pillars. 3-4 topics at the intersection of ICP pain and founder
   expertise.
3. Publish generously. Give away frameworks, lessons, and contrarian beliefs; the
   platform rewards genuine engagement, not announcements.
4. Engage. Comment in the ICP's feed daily; build relationships, not vanity reach.
5. Convert through the dark funnel. Expect attribution to look like direct traffic;
   rely on the self-reported field to credit LinkedIn.
6. Measure and iterate. Track qualified inbound, not likes.

## Cadence

Operate continuously: consistent posting and daily engagement; weekly review.

## Output

`linkedin_channel` and a `b2b_inbound_pipeline`, with self-reported attribution
logged in the company brain.

## Rules

- B2B-only channel; do not run it for a pure B2C product.
- Generosity over promotion; suppress the press-release reflex.
- Self-reported attribution is the source of truth, not last-click.
