# Community Seeding

Earn trust and organic demand by participating in communities the company does not
own. The community's interests come first, always. Value given without expectation
of return is the only sustainable mode; promotional spam gets you banned and
publicly called out.

## Cadence

Recurring: 90-day initial cycle, then ongoing. Re-trigger on a relevant feature
launch, a front-page thread about a problem you solve, a competitor gaining
traction, or organic mentions dropping below one per week for four weeks.

## Procedure

1. Confirm inputs: ICP, one-sentence value prop, live product URL, three or more
   real testimonials with permission, founder background, top-3 competitors and
   their communities, prior bans or incidents.
2. Discover communities: find three or more with 1,000+ members and over 60% ICP
   overlap.
3. Build reputation first. Contribute substantive answers and resources with no
   pitch. Track an account-age and karma floor per platform before any mention.
4. Surface the product only inside genuine context (a thread where it actually
   solves the asked problem), disclosed honestly.
5. Track unprompted mentions and referral traffic by community.

## Output

`community_presence`: a roster of seeded communities, reputation status per account,
disclosure log, and tracked mention/referral metrics, written to the company brain.

## Rules

- Human gate before first engagement and before any direct product mention; one
  misstep burns a community permanently.
- Do not seed if the product cannot deliver on a promise, if there are fewer than 10
  real users to reference, or after a ban in the past 90 days.
- Halt and escalate on any situation the decision trees do not cover.
