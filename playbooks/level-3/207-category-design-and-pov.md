# Category Design and Point of View

## Procedure
1. Name the old game. Write one paragraph describing the category buyers currently put you in, the metrics they use to compare vendors, and why those metrics reward incumbents. This is the frame you intend to break.
2. Find the shift. Identify the structural change in technology, behavior, or economics that makes the old frame obsolete. State it as a dated trend with two pieces of evidence. Your point of view is only credible if it rides a real shift, not a wish.
3. Write the contrarian thesis. In three sentences assert what most people in the market believe, why it is now wrong, and what they should believe instead. Name the enemy: an outdated approach, not a competitor by name. This becomes pov_thesis.
4. Name the new category. Generate eight to ten candidate names, then test each against three filters: a buyer can repeat it after hearing it once, it is not already owned by a competitor, and it implies your strengths. Pick one and write a one-line definition stating what it is, who it is for, and the new metric it should be judged on.
5. Build the proof spine. List three claims that must be true for the category to matter, and the asset that proves each (a benchmark, a customer outcome, a teardown). Schedule one to publish this week.

## Output
A two-page category_definition plus a sharable pov_thesis with named enemy, new metric, and three proof points.
