# Integration & Co-marketing

Integration and co-marketing (Ecosystem-Led Growth) is a structural engine, not a
supplementary channel. Partner-led deals close 53% faster at 72% lower CAC; products
with 4+ integrations retain 18 to 22% better. The mechanism is trust transfer: a buyer
who trusts Partner A, deeply integrated with you, partially transfers that trust.
Partnerships that exist only on paper generate no revenue; the activation layer is
where most of the work goes.

## Procedure

1. Pre-flight checklist (escalate if fewer than four of six hold): ICP defined with
   50+ tagged closed-won accounts, a documented integration use case, confirmed
   engineering bandwidth, CRM partner-tracking fields, a warmed outreach domain
   (>= 90-day history, < 0.5% spam), and a named human signatory.
2. Map the partner landscape across four types (workflow integrators, data complements,
   platform ecosystems, distribution partners); find 10+ candidates per type via CRM
   tech-stack data, G2/Capterra, and Zapier directory.
3. Score and prioritize on shared-customer overlap and strategic fit; build a joint-value pitch.
4. Ship the integration (native or no-code/iPaaS if bandwidth is tight) and list it in
   the relevant marketplaces.
5. Activate: webinars, co-branded content, joint customer campaigns; embed the
   integration in onboarding to drive adoption.
6. Track partner-sourced/influenced pipeline and integration adoption; kill if zero
   qualified opps and < 5% adoption after 90 days.

## Output

`integration_partnerships`: signed agreements, live integrations, co-marketing assets,
and tracked partner pipeline, recorded in the company brain.

## Rules

- Recurring. Run 2 to 3 partner cycles (8 to 16 weeks each) in parallel.
- Human gate on partnership agreements (named signatory required).
- Activation, not paper. A signed deal with no co-marketing produces no revenue.
