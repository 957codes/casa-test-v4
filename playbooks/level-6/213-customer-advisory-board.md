# Customer Advisory Board

## Procedure
1. Define the board's mandate in one page: what decisions it informs (roadmap sequencing, pricing direction, new segments), what it does not (support escalation, individual feature requests). Set cadence (quarterly), size (8 to 12 members), and term length (one year). Save this as the advisory board charter.
2. Select members from your ICP account list. Target a spread across segment, company size, and lifecycle stage, and require an executive sponsor plus a hands-on practitioner per account. Bias toward engaged accounts who give candid feedback over your largest logos. Draft a personal invite from a founder, framing it as influence, not a sales ask.
3. Build the first session agenda before inviting: 20 minutes of your roadmap thesis, 40 minutes of structured discussion on two or three real tradeoffs, 15 minutes of open signal. Send a short pre-read 48 hours ahead so members arrive prepared.
4. Run the session with a dedicated notetaker. Capture every steer as a discrete item with the member, the decision it touches, and the strength of opinion. Convert these into advisory roadmap feedback the product team triages within one week.
5. Close the loop. Within ten business days, send each member what you heard and what you changed. Ask the most positive members to join your reference and advocate list for case studies, intros, and references.

## Output
A standing advisory board with a charter, a tracked feedback log, and a named advocate roster, refreshed each quarter.
