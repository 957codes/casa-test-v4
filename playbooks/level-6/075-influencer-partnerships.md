# Influencer Partnerships

Influencer marketing runs on parasocial trust transfer: a creator's recommendation
carries the credibility of a trusted friend. It fails in most campaigns for three
reasons: the audience had no latent demand, content was over-scripted so the ad-detector
fired, or there was no conversion path. A documented DTC case had a 3.4% hit rate (11 of
327); the wins were micro-creators in tight niches with authentic fit and clear CTAs.

## Procedure

1. Confirm inputs: ICP, campaign objective, budget, product for seeding, legal/claim
   constraints, brand safety rules.
2. Build the three-tier stack and allocate by objective: Tier 1 cultural anchors
   (legitimacy, not conversion), Tier 2 niche authorities (the conversion workhorse),
   Tier 3 nano/UGC (volume, social proof, paid amplification source).
3. Select platforms by audience fit, then scout and vet creators on audience alignment
   and engagement, scoring each.
4. Contract with explicit deliverables, usage rights, and FTC disclosure; brief without
   over-scripting; ensure a dedicated conversion path (landing page, promo code, UTM).
5. Measure with incrementality (codes, holdouts), not vanity EMV; reinvest in winners.

## Output

`influencer_program`: a vetted creator roster, signed contracts, live tracked content,
and an incrementality report with reinvestment recommendation, recorded in the company brain.

## Rules

- Recurring. Always-on demand engine; audit roster and outreach monthly.
- Human gate on contracts, spend, and brand-safety calls.
- Audience fit over reach. A misaligned macro creator wastes budget; do not over-script.
